  1. To swim, to dive into the fire, to ride
    On the curled clouds, [1]
    Whose speechless song being many, seems one. [2]
  2. Time doth transfix the flourish set on youth, [3]
    Which bounteous gift thou shouldst in bounty cherish. [4]
  3. Be collected:
    No more amazement: tell your piteous heart [5]
    Thou art thy mother’s glass, and she in thee. [6]
  4. So, ere you find where light in darkness lies, [7]
    Gentle breath of yours my sails must fill. [8]
  5. Grant, if thou wilt, thou art beloved of many, [9]
    And pray, and sing, and tell old tales, and laugh
    At gilded butterflies. [10]
  6. Now my charms are all o’erthrown, [11]
    Begot of nothing but vain fantasy. [12]
  7. Look, whom she best endow’d she gave thee more; [13]
    Our fancies are more giddy and unfirm. [14]
  8. Sap checked with frost and lusty leaves quite gone, [15]
    Courage and hope both teaching him the practice. [16]
  9. Rough winds do shake the darling buds of May, [17]
    I’ll kneel down, and ask of thee forgiveness. [18]
  10. Seems, madam! nay it is; I know not ‘seems.’ [19]
    All the world’s a stage. [20]
  11. The moon new-bent in heaven, shall behold the night [21]
    That has such people in’t! [22]
  12. And having climb’d the steep-up heavenly hill, [23]
    Fortune, good night: smile once more: turn thy wheel! [24]
  13. Beauty o’ersnow’d and bareness every where [25]
    Thaws and resolves itself into a dew. [26]
  14. Their eyes do offices of truth, their words are natural breath, [27]
    all dedicated to closeness and the bettering of my mind. [28]
  15. Sound me from my lowest note to the top of my compass [29]
    My heart is true as steel. [30]
  16. Thy self thy foe, to thy sweet self too cruel: [31]
    I am sure care’s an enemy to life. [32]
  17. You have often
    Begun to tell me what I am, but stopp’d [33]
    Conceal me what I am, and be my aid. [34]
  18. Have more than thou showest,
    Speak less than thou knowest, [35]
    Nor lose possession of that fair thou ow’st. [36]
  19. What seest thou else
    In the dark backward and abysm of time? [37]
    So full of shapes is fancy
    That it alone is high fantastical. [38]
  20. To give away yourself, keeps yourself still,
    And you must live, drawn by your own sweet skill. [39]
  21. Those be rubies, fairy favors. [40]
    They sparkle still the right Promethean fire. [41]
  22. Men must endure their going hence, even as their coming hither; [42]
    I am a fool to weep at what I am glad of. [43]
  23. Get thee to a nunnery: [44]
    A contract of true love to celebrate. [45]
  24. If music be the food of love, play on, [46]
    And let this world no longer be a stage. [47]
  25. A man may see how this world goes with no eyes. Look with thine ears: [48]
    The murmuring surge, that on the unnumber’d idle pebbles chafes. [49]
  26. No traveller returns, puzzles the will [50]
    If it be not to come, it will be now. [51]
  27. How beauteous mankind is! O brave new world, [52]
    Merrily, merrily, shall I live now. [53]
  28. These our actors,
    As I foretold you, were all spirits and [54]
    Do make me give the lie to my true sight. [55]
  29. With the help of your good hands. [56]
    All things in common nature should produce
    Without sweat or endeavour. [57]
  30. And some donation freely to estate [58]
    Under the blossom that hangs on the bough. [59]
  31. Sounds and sweet airs, that give delight and hurt not [60]
    In a cowslip’s bell I lie. [61]
  32. But that the dread of something after death, [62]
    It droppeth as the gentle rain from heaven. [63]
  33. And ye that on the sands with printless foot do chase the ebbing Neptune, [64]
    O, let me see thee walk. Thou dost not halt. [65]
  34. But, like a cloistress, she will veiled walk [66]
    Out of the jaws of death. [67]
  35. Misery acquaints a man with strange bed-fellows [68]
    Which are the children of an idle brain. [69]
  36. For who would bear the whips and scorns of time, [70]
    That show, contain and nourish all the world. [71]
  37. Often have you heard that told: [72]
    Wherefore are these things hid? wherefore have
    these gifts a curtain before ‘em? [73]
  38. And summer’s lease hath all too short a date: [74]
    The hour’s now come;
    The very minute bids thee ope thine ear. [75]
  39. I am all the daughters of my father’s house,
    And all the brothers too. [76]
    O spirit of love! how quick and fresh art thou. [77]
  40. And enterprises of great pith and moment [78]
    Are melted into air, into thin air. [79]
  41. O, swear not by the moon, the inconstant moon, [80]
    If it be not now, yet it will come: the readiness is all. [81]
  42. There’s no harm done. [82]
    Speak what we feel, not what we ought to say. [83]
  43. Youth’s a stuff will not endure. [84]
    Out of that no hope what great hope have you! [85]
  44. And thus the native hue of resolution [86]
    Strikes where it doth love. [87]
  45. Gentles, do not reprehend: [88] nothing but to show you
    How a king may go a progress through the guts of a beggar. [89]
  46. Nature’s bequest gives nothing but doth lend, [90]
    Thy truth, then, be thy dower. [91]
  47. Study is like the heaven’s glorious sun, [92]
    Which touch’d the very virtue of compassion in thee. [93]
  48. Uttering such dulcet and harmonious breath [94]
    That long have frown’d upon their enmity! [95]
  49. Give me your hands, if we be friends, [96]
    We are such stuff as dreams are made on. [97]
  50. And nothing ‘gainst Time’s scythe can make defence. [98]
    Herein lives wisdom, beauty and increase. [99]
  51. I must go seek some dewdrops here [100]
    It blesseth him that gives and him that takes. [101]
  52. Let Hercules himself do what he may, [102]
    If you pardon, we will mend. [103]
  53. Now stand you on the top of happy hours, [104]
    Against the stormy gusts of winter’s day. [105]
  54. And every fair from fair sometime declines, [106]
    So we grow together, [107] thorough bush, thorough brier. [108]
  55. To take arms against a sea of troubles,
    And by opposing end them? [109]
    We will unite the white rose and the red. [110]
  56. These most brisk and giddy-paced times: [111]
    Is man no more than this? Consider him well. [112]
  57. Who with thy saffron wings upon my flowers [113]
    Calls back the lovely April of her prime: [114]
    The form of my intent. [115]
  58. It is a wise father that knows his own child. [116]
    Like to a double cherry, seeming parted,
    But yet an union in partition. [117]
  59. In action how like an angel! In apprehension how like a god! [118]
    That the rude sea grew civil at her song. [119]
  60. Gaze where you should, and that will clear your sight, [120]
    Enrich the time to come with smooth-fac’d peace. [121]
  61. The slings and arrows of outrageous fortune; [122]
    These blessed candles of the night. [123]
  62. O, from what power hast thou this powerful might, [124]
    By chance or nature’s changing course untrimm’d? [125]
  63. Rise from the ground like feathered Mercury, [126]
    Then to the elements be free, and fare thou well! [127]
  64. My spirits, as in a dream, are all bound up. [128]
    Diffusest honey-drops, refreshing showers. [129]
  65. For never-resting time leads summer on [130]
    The wheel is come full circle: I am here. [131]
  66. The undiscover’d country from whose bourn no traveller returns, [132]
    Two lovely berries moulded on one stem. [133]
  67. They are the books, the arts, the academes, [134]
    And I serve the fairy queen. [135]
  68. Smooth runs the water where the brook is deep. [136]
    What stronger breastplate than a heart untainted? [137]
  69. Then wisely, good sir, weigh our sorrow with our comfort, [138]
    that ebb and flow by the moon. [139]
  70. All that glisters is not gold, [140]
    To plainness honour’s bound when majesty falls to folly. [141]
  71. Think’st thou I’d make a life of jealousy? [142]
    The quality of mercy is not strain’d. [143]
  72. O heaven, O earth, bear witness to this sound, [144]
    As full of spirit as the month of May. [145]
  73. When I consider every thing that grows
    Holds in perfection but a little moment, [146]
    Pray you, tread softly, that the blind mole may not
    Hear a foot fall. [147]
  74. With gentle conference, soft and affable. [148]
    Let your indulgence set me free. [149]
  75. Light, seeking light, doth light of light beguile; [150]
    Now let not Nature’s hand keep the wild flood confin’d! [151]
  76. True, I talk of dreams, [152] for there is nothing
    Either good or bad, but thinking makes it so. [153]
  77. What’s in a name? that which we call a rose [154]
    Being once display’d doth fall that very hour. [155]
  78. I’ll teach you how to flow. [156]
    O, if you but knew how you the purpose cherish. [157]
  79. What is love? ‘tis not hereafter; [158]
    And being frank she lends to those are free. [159]
  80. What’s to come is still unsure: [160] what’s past is prologue, [161]
    Present mirth hath present laughter. [162]
  81. But thy eternal summer shall not fade [163]
    Is it a world to hide virtues in? [164]
  82. I, thus neglecting worldly ends, [165]
    Play out the play. [166]
  83. I stumbled when I saw: [167] If it be now, ‘tis not to come; [168]
    Our means secure us, and our mere defects prove our commodities. [169]
  84. As painfully to pore upon a book to seek the light of truth, [170]
    out of this nettle, danger, we pluck this flower, safety. [171]
  85. Defer no time, delays have dangerous ends; [172]
    Thou shalt be as free as mountain winds. [173]
  86. Understanding begins to swell [174] by prayer,
    Which pierces so that it assaults
    Mercy itself, and frees all faults. [175]
  87. With smiling plenty, and fair prosperous days! [176]
    What doth gravity out of his bed at midnight? [177]
  88. Th’ endeavour of this present breath may buy [178]
    The very lifeblood of our enterprise. [179]
  89. But I will tarry; the fool will stay,
    And let the wise man fly: [180]
    To pay this debt of love but to a brother. [181]
  90. And now let’s go hand in hand, not one before another,
    Swifter than the moon’s sphere. [182]
  91. Smiling at grief, [183] awake, awake! [184]
    In delay there lies no plenty. [185]
  92. And then the moon, like to a silver bow [186]
    Upon the place beneath: it is twice blest. [187]
  93. And, like bright metal on a sullen ground, [188]
    Consideration like an angel came. [189]
  94. When we have shuffled off this mortal coil, [190]
    There’s nothing ill can dwell in such a temple. [191]
  95. Be not afeard; the isle is full of noises, [192]
    To entrap the wisest. [193]
  96. And as the morning steals upon the night, [194]
    Make me a willow cabin at your gate. [195]
  97. Make the babbling gossip of the air cry out: [196]
    There is occasions and causes why and wherefore in all things. [197]
  98. Or to thyself at least kind-hearted prove: [198]
    As fast as thou shalt wane, so fast thou growest. [199]
  99. Halloo your name to the reverberate hills [200]
    And deeper than did ever plummet sound I’ll drown my book. [201]
  100. If of life you keep a care,
    Shake off slumber, and beware. [202]
    You rub the sore,
    When you should bring the plaster. [203]
  101. Thy virtues spoke of, and thy beauty sounded, [204]
    The better part of valour is discretion. [205]
  102. Draw the curtain close and let us all to meditation, [206]
    To pluck bright honour from the pale-fac’d moon. [207]
  103. Their rising senses begin to chase
    The ignorant fumes that mantle their clearer reason. [208]
    It is the quality o’ th’ climate. [209]
  104. O spirit of love, how quick and fresh art thou! [210]
    Few in millions can speak like us. [211]
  105. While you live, tell truth and shame the devil! [212]
    Strive mightily, but eat and drink as friends. [213]
  106. Burd’ned with like weight of pain, [214]

  107. He weeds the corn, and still lets grow the weeding. [216]
    In brief, sir, study what you most affect. [217]
  108. So shaken as we are, so wan with care, [218]
    Awake, dear heart, awake; thou hast slept well; Awake. [219]
